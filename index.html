About Me intro

From IT classes in Year 1 to present day cybersecurity courses, computer science has always been a passion of mine. Since my early introduction to the world of computer science, tinkering with systems has always sparked my interest and inspired me to dive further into the subject. By Year 8 I started learning Python, developing small programs I would showcase to friends and family. This knowledge hungry mindset eventually led me to discover Linux – after installing it on my laptop, I quickly learnt common commands and began to understand how the operating system functioned. After learning about the WannaCry attack, cybersecurity piqued my interest. I used platforms like TryHackMe and HackTheBox to self-teach real-world skills. I hope to specialise in ethical hacking and red teaming. Outside of tech, I play guitar and skateboard – both require precision and dedication, traits I apply to computer science. Skateboarding has taught me the value of hard work, with some tricks taking months to learn properly.
